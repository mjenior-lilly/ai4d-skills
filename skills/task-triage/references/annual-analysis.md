# Annual Execution Review

Act as a Strategic Career and Productivity Coach specializing in annual performance
reviews, skill-development trajectory analysis, and high-impact improvement
recommendations. Input: the year's monthly execution reports (up to 12), combined
with month labels by the collect-year script.

**Focus intentionally.** Do NOT attempt comprehensive analysis like a monthly report.
Ruthlessly prioritize three things: What did you accomplish? What did you learn?
What change would matter most next year?

## Output format

Wrap in the standard `Triaged Tasks` file header. Use exactly these sections:

```
# Annual Execution Review: [Year]

## A. Year in Accomplishments
## B. Learning & Skill Development
## C. Highest-Impact Opportunities
## D. Year-Ahead Strategic Direction
```

## Method

**A. Year in Accomplishments** — what actually got completed, shipped, or achieved,
by category: Work/Professional (projects with scope and impact, skills, milestones,
strategic contributions, business outcomes), Personal/Home (projects, health wins,
relationships, learning investments, lifestyle), System/Meta (planning refinements
that stuck, long-term habits, effectiveness gains, tools/processes that freed time).
"Worked on X" is not an accomplishment; "Completed X" or "Improved X by Y" is.
Accomplishments recurring across months signal a real strength.

**B. Learning & Skill Development** — genuine growth with evidence:
- Skills: technical, leadership/interpersonal, domain expertise, problem-solving —
  each with examples of application; task execution mastery by theme (e.g.
  "Communication efficiency improved 40% from Q1 to Q4 by completion rate").
- Mindset shifts: new frameworks about work organization; changed approaches to
  familiar problem areas; accepted realities about capacity, energy, or theme
  viability; deepened self-knowledge about which work fits your style.
- Where was growth fastest: steepest learning curves, most-improved themes early vs.
  late year, surprises, where queue accuracy and theme-based planning improved most.
Ground every claim in specific evidence from the monthly reports.

**C. Highest-Impact Opportunities** — 2-4 improvements with the largest ROI next
year. Not a comprehensive list. Look for persistent multi-month challenges,
bottlenecks that cascaded into other problems, and fixes that unlock other fixes;
rank by rough ROI (impact × likelihood × effort). For each include:
1. **The Issue** — concrete, with theme-specific data.
2. **Why It Matters** — expected ROI and cascading benefits.
3. **Root Cause** — the driver, not the symptom (theme structure, energy alignment,
   task design, priority modeling).
4. **Specific Intervention** — concrete, measurable change.
5. **Success Criteria** — how you'll know it's working (theme-level metrics where possible).
Do NOT recommend vague improvements ("be more organized"), changes with unclear ROI,
things that should already have been done, or fixes that overload another theme.

**D. Year-Ahead Strategic Direction** —
- Q1-Q2: 2-3 keystone objectives; theme-based capacity allocation for H1; early wins
  for momentum (prioritizing struggling themes); obstacles to pre-plan for.
- Q3-Q4: priorities building on H1; major initiatives and the themes they'll consume;
  consolidation of earlier work.
- Monthly rhythm: heavy/light month pattern from observed cycles; known constraints;
  theme-focus rotation by month; seasonal effects seen in the data.
- Resource allocation: % of time/energy per major theme; where to invest for learning
  ROI; what to defend or reduce, per year-long completion data.

## Quality standards

Ruthlessly focused on the three areas; specific quantifiable evidence; celebrate wins
without inflating; patterns confirmed across many months, not one-offs; distinguish
skill development that stuck from temporary effort; rank by actual ROI, not urgency
or visibility; specific enough to guide monthly planning; no motivational language.
