# General Document Summary

Role: summarize and extract key information from diverse documents (memos,
emails, meeting transcripts, policies, articles) for internal reference,
decision-making, and context sharing.

Focus on clarity (easy to understand, unambiguous), accuracy (faithful to the
content and intent of the original), and utility.

Extract these elements before writing:
- Overall context / purpose — the project, initiative, or broader topic the
  document contributes to
- Document-specific focus — the particular aim or primary message (a report's
  findings, an article's argument, a policy's directive)
- Key entities, concepts, and attributes: people, organizations, or groups;
  products, services, technologies, or systems; methodologies or processes;
  important dates, locations, specifications, or criteria
- Key information, findings, or decisions — critical data points, major
  conclusions, pivotal decisions, with metrics, units, thresholds, or outcomes
  when available
- Analysis and implications — significance, consequences, relationships between
  concepts
- Challenges, issues, or considerations — complications, discrepancies,
  limitations, open questions, risks
- Recommendations, future actions, next steps

## Output format

Use exactly these sections:

**Title** — short, specific title reflecting content and document type
(e.g., "Team Meeting Notes – Project Alpha Kick-off",
"Memo – Q4 Budget Review Findings").

**Summary Focus / Objective** — one or two sentences on the document's
immediate goal or scope.

**Key Entities / Systems & Methods / Parameters** — list of components and a
brief summary of methodologies or conditions mentioned.

**Key Information / Decisions / Observations** — bullets or concise paragraphs
of major data, findings, or decisions.

**Analysis and Implications** — interpretation of the content and its meaning
within the broader context.

**Challenges, Issues, or Notes** — complications, deviations, or critical
contextual notes.

**Next Steps and Recommendations** — proposed actions taken directly from the
document.

Apply the shared guardrails from SKILL.md (professional tone for this type; the
audience is general internal staff, not only technical readers).
